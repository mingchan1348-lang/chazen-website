Chapter 05 / The Song Room

The Song Room

宋室

A quiet exhibition on Song dynasty tea aesthetics: foam, dark ceramic, scholar restraint, and the luxury of saying almost nothing.

Exhibition Thesis

Restraint is not emptiness. It is concentration.

foam / black glaze / breath / silence

01

點茶

Dian Cha

Powdered tea is whisked into a pale foam. The act is controlled, quiet, and almost architectural.

02

建盞

Jian Ware

Dark bowls reveal light foam. Hare's fur, oil spot, and kiln fire become part of the ceremony.

03

茶筅

Tea Whisk

The whisk is a discipline of rhythm: wrist, breath, texture, then stillness.

04

文人茶

Scholar Studio

Tea belongs beside ink, paper, stone, poetry, and the measured life of the desk.

Curator Note

Song elegance lives in what is removed.

No loud ornament, no theatrical abundance. The room asks visitors to look at a bowl until the bowl becomes weather, time, hand, fire, and breath.
